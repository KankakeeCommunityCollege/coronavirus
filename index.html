Coronavirus (COVID-19) Response

Watch now: Message to students as online-based coursework begins 3/23/20
Watch now: Dr. Boyd extends work-from-home directive for faculty and staff 4/1/20

KCC is committed to reducing the potential spread of coronavirus (COVID-19). Following are statuses of KCC areas. Each status includes the last time it was updated.

  • KCC related cases of COVID-19: If KCC receives information about confirmed cases, the college will notify employees and students who were reported to be in contact with the person in the 14 days prior to the positive test date. Any employees/students notified should self-isolate and monitor themselves for symptoms. (Updated 4/1/20, 2 p.m.)
  • College facilities: Closed through May 30, or until Illinois' disaster proclamation is lifted. Student support services like tutoring, advising, counseling and registration are still available online. (Updated, 4/24/20, 2 p.m.)
  • Credit classes: Will be taught online through the end of the Summer 2020 term (July 23). Each professor/instructor will communicate with their students regarding how their specific classes will proceed in an online environment. Some labs will meet online. Clinicals will continue as allowed by the host site. (Updated, 3/26/20, 10 a.m.)
  • Grading options for Spring 2020: To provide students with greater flexibility following the move to remote instruction due to the COVID-19 pandemic, KCC is offering alternative grading options to all Spring 2020 courses except those which ended prior to Spring Break. (Added 4/29/20, 5 p.m.)
  • Adult education: Classes suspended through May 30. (Updated, 4/23/20, 4:30 p.m.)
  • Continuing education: Online classes still offered. Face-to-face classes canceled through May 30. (Updated, 4/23/20, 4:30 p.m.)
  • Advising/Registration: Summer registration is available online for returning students.
    • Advisors for new and returning students are available by phone Monday through Friday 8 a.m. - 5 p.m. To have an advisor call you, submit the Advising Call Request Form and an advisor will call back within two business days.
    • Returning students can make an appointment for a specific day or time. 

    • General questions can be emailed to advising@kcc.edu. (Updated, 3/16/20, 3 p.m.) 

  • Health Careers program applicants: See Information for Health Careers Applicants while KCC facilities are closed.
  • Bookstore, Food Pantry, Fitness Center: Closed/unavailable until college facilities re-open. (Updated, 3/20/20, 4 p.m.)
  • Tutoring: Online Writing Studio is available through your course in Canvas. Choose the Online Writing Studio tab. (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • Events sponsored by KCC: All college events, including KCC events at off-campus venues, are canceled/postponed until after the Illinois' “shelter in place” order is lifted. Event organizers, including college sponsors of external groups, will inform those who were invited that their event has been canceled. This includes outside organizers renting space at KCC. (Updated, 3/20/20, 4 p.m.)
  • Athletic games and practices: Spring sports seasons (softball and baseball) are canceled. Women's basketball NJCAA Div. II national tournament is canceled. (Updated 3/17/20, 11 a.m.)
  • Employees: Please work with your supervisor to determine if you will work remotely. (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • College-sponsored student travel: Suspended. (Updated March 13, 1 p.m. – to include athletics)

More information

  • Information and an application for funds through the Federal Emergency Stimulus Funding (CARES Act) are available through the Office of Financial Aid.
  • Online learning support is offered through Canvas (KCC’s online course management system). Email questions to canvashelp@kcc.edu.
  • Xfinity is opening its WiFi network for free, along with other opportunities to be connected to the internet. This information is posted as a public service. Comcast/Xfinity is not affiliated with KCC.

Shelter-In-Place Order Extended

POSTED ON: 04/24/20

Hello, Students

With Illinois' stay-at-home order extended, KCC's facilities will remain closed through May 30, but our faculty and staff are available to make sure you get the classes and services you need.

During the last five weeks, we know you have made many sacrifices to stay on course and meet your college goals. As the spring semester moves into May, the college faculty and staff remain available to assist in any way.

More than 400 KCC students are still on track to graduate in just three weeks. You're almost there! Congratulations! Our graduates are just one example of what it means to be #strongtogether! KCC Graduation Spirit week will start May 11 – and will include an online commencement program May 15 beginning at 4 p.m.

Looking ahead, we sincerely hope that COVID-19 does not derail your educational goals. KCC is dedicated to helping you stay on track. Summer classes will begin June 1 online. Registration is now available for both summer and fall. If you want to talk to your advisor about how to keep moving forward, even with other things going on, returning students can make an appointment online, or send an Advising Call Request and an advisor will call back within two business days. Financial Aid options are still available for summer and fall.

As we have since mid-March, you can visit coronavirus.kcc.edu to get further information on KCC's actions Please let us know how we can help you be as successful as possible.
