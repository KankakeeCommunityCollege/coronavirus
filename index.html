Coronavirus (COVID-19) Response

KCC is committed to reducing the potential spread of coronavirus (COVID-19). Following are statuses of KCC areas. Each status includes the last time it was updated.

  • KCC related cases of COVID-19 (coronavirus): None reported. (Updated 3/19/20, 11 a.m.)
  • College facilities: Closed March 18-30. Student support services like tutoring, advising, counseling and registration are still available online. (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • Credit classes: Will be taught online through the end of the Spring 2020 semester (May 14). Each professor/instructor will communicate with their students regarding how their specific classes will proceed in an online environment. Some labs will meet online. Clinicals will continue as allowed by the host site. (Updated, 3/20/20, 10 a.m.)
  • Adult education: Classes suspended through May 14. (Updated, 3/20/20, 10 a.m.)
  • Continuing education: Classes canceled through May 14 with the exception of online offerings. (Updated, 3/20/20, 10 a.m.)
  • Advising/Registration: Summer registration is available online for returning students.
    • Advisors for new and returning students are available by phone Monday through Friday 8 a.m. - 5 p.m. To have an advisor call you, submit the Advising Call Request Form and an advisor will call back within two business days.
    • Returning students can make an appointment for a specific day or time. 

    • General questions can be emailed to advising@kcc.edu. (Updated, 3/16/20, 3 p.m.) 

  • KCC Foundation: Women’s Giving Circle event on April 20 is canceled. (Updated, 3/20/20, 10 a.m.)
  • Bookstore: The KCC Hammes Bookstore will be open 10 a.m. - 6 p.m. on March 17, 18 and 19. It will serve as a pick-up point for technology, books and supplies that students need. (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • Food Pantry: Unavailable after March 19... Care packages available for pick-up until 6 p.m. March 18, then 10 a.m. - 6 p.m. on March 19. (Updated, 3/18/20, 2 p.m.)
  • Tutoring: Online Writing Studio is available through your course in Canvas. Choose the Online Writing Studio tab. (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • Events sponsored by KCC: All college events, including KCC events at off-campus venues, are canceled through April 1. Some events in April and May have also been canceled. Event organizers, including college sponsors of external groups, will inform those who were invited that their event has been canceled. This includes outside organizers renting space at KCC. (Updated, 3/19/20, 3 p.m.)
  • Fitness Center: Closed through March 30 (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • Athletic games and practices: Spring sports seasons (softball and baseball) are canceled. Women's basketball NJCAA Div. II national tournament is canceled. (Updated 3/17/20, 11 a.m.)
  • Employees: Please work with your supervisor to determine if you will work remotely. (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • College-sponsored student travel: Suspended. (Updated March 13, 1 p.m. – to include athletics)

Message to KCC students

POSTED ON: 03/20/20

Students:

As conditions continue to evolve, and cautionary measures remain necessary, Kankakee Community College will continue with online classes through the conclusion of the Spring 2020 semester (May 14). This is later than the April 20 date communicated last week. This effort is to continue the college’s commitment to reducing potential pathways for community spread of coronavirus (COVID-19).

Each professor/instructor will continue to communicate with their students through Canvas - the online learning environment. Classes and support services, such as the library and tutoring, will continue to be available online.

Adult Education and GED preparation courses are suspended through the end of Spring 2020 semester. Continuing Education offerings are canceled with the exception of its online offerings.

KCC facilities remain closed through March 30. The facilities closure includes the Riverfront Campus and Manufacturing and Industrial Technology Center in Kankakee, the North Extension Center in Bradley, and the South Extension Center in Watseka.

KCC will update coronavirus.kcc.edu as the main source of information for our college’s actions and response.

The health and safety of our faculty, staff, students and community is a priority. The college’s administration will continue to monitor the state’s recommendations and consult with public health authorities.

This is hard. It cannot be denied. KCC is committed to providing the high level of instructional rigor, service and support that you have come to expect. We just need to work together, course-by-course and sometimes person-by-person. It may take some time to adjust to Canvas and online learning if you’re new to it, but we are here for you. Please let us know how we can help you be as successful as possible in your courses.

Sincerely,

Dr. Michael Boyd
