Students must follow the vaccination policy—or test weekly

All college services are available. Please understand that because of the changing nature of COVID-19 and Illinois’ Reopening Plan, this information may change without notice. If you don’t see what you are looking for—or would like more information—please contact us.

Vaccination policy

KCC complies with vaccine and mask requirements from the state of Illinois and other governmental agencies, as appropriate. The vaccination policy applies to those who are inside a college facility for more than 15 minutes at least once a week on a regular basis, including students and employees.

The FAQ page has a special section dedicated to questions about the vaccination policy.

  • If you are already vaccinated, complete KCC’s verification form online. This is a one-time verification. 

Vaccination Verification Form

  • If you are not already vaccinated, consider making plans to receive the first dose of a two-dose COVID-19 vaccination series or a single-dose vaccination. Second doses of the vaccine must be received within 30 days after the first dose.
Weekly COVID-19 tests

If you have not received a COVID-19 vaccine, you must be tested for COVID-19 once per week in order to visit a KCC facility. The deadline to submit results is 11:59 p.m. each Saturday. 

  • The final day to use SHIELD Testing at KCC is July 13.
  • Walk-ins and appointments are both welcome. The location is the second floor of the D building, in the hallway East of the Student Affairs entrance (Room D222). You can also ask for directions at the Student Affairs reception desk.
Visiting the college
  • KCC's opening status and guidelines consider federal, state, and local directives. The college also follows Illinois Community College Board and Restore Illinois guidelines.
  • Masks are optional for everyone, regardless of COVID-19 vaccination status. 
  • Maintain physical distancing while utilizing services and common areas at the college.
  • Follow all instructions on posted signage and from staff.
  • Classes are offered in-person, online and a hybrid (some online, some in-person). The class schedule has details on how the class is being offered.
  • College facilities are open. There is free wi-fi throughout the college. Students can use KCC's Library or any other appropriate college space to take part in online classes, or to study—even if the course is not being held in-person. 
  • Deep cleaning and disinfecting of frequently used and “high touch” spaces continues at all facilities.
Coming to KCC after COVID-19 diagnosis or exposure

As recommended by the Kankakee County Health Department, KCC is following the Center for Disease Control quarantine and isolation instructions: 

  • If you test positive for COVID-19, isolate for 5 days after onset of symptoms, or 5 days from specimen collection date if no symptoms are present. Day 0 is the first day of symptoms or specimen collection date if asymptomatic. If you continue to have fever or other symptoms, or have not improved after 5 days of isolation, do not end isolation until you are fever-free for 24 hours and your other symptoms have improved. 
  • Continue to wear a well-fitted mask around others for 5 additional days after release from isolation. If unable to wear a mask when around others, continue to isolate for a total of 10 days. 
    • Avoid being around students or staff who are immunocompromised or at high risk for severe disease. 
    • If masks need to be removed (e.g., for lunch), ensure 6 feet of physical distancing until 10 days after symptom onset or positive test. 

All college services are available
