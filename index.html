Coronavirus (COVID-19) Response

Watch now: Message to students as online-based coursework begins 3/23/20
Watch now: Dr. Boyd extends work-from-home directive for faculty and staff 4/1/20

KCC is committed to reducing the potential spread of coronavirus (COVID-19). Following are statuses of KCC areas. Each status includes the last time it was updated.

  • KCC related cases of COVID-19 (coronavirus): If KCC receives information about confirmed cases, the college will notify employees and students who were reported to be in contact with the person in the 14 days prior to the positive test date. Any employees/students notified should self-isolate and monitor themselves for symptoms. (Updated 4/1/20, 2 p.m.)
  • College facilities: Closed through May 30, or until Illinois’ stay at home order is lifted. Student support services like tutoring, advising, counseling and registration are still available online. (Updated, 4/23/20, 4:30 p.m.)
  • Credit classes: Will be taught online through the end of the Summer 2020 term (July 23). Each professor/instructor will communicate with their students regarding how their specific classes will proceed in an online environment. Some labs will meet online. Clinicals will continue as allowed by the host site. (Updated, 3/26/20, 10 a.m.)
  • Adult education: Classes suspended through May 30. (Updated, 4/23/20, 04:30 p.m.)
  • Continuing education: Online classes still offered. Face-to-face classes canceled through May 30. (Updated, 4/23/20, 4:30 p.m.)
  • Advising/Registration: Summer registration is available online for returning students.
    • Advisors for new and returning students are available by phone Monday through Friday 8 a.m. - 5 p.m. To have an advisor call you, submit the Advising Call Request Form and an advisor will call back within two business days.
    • Returning students can make an appointment for a specific day or time. 

    • General questions can be emailed to advising@kcc.edu. (Updated, 3/16/20, 3 p.m.) 

  • Health Careers program applicants: See Information for Health Careers Applicants while KCC facilities are closed.
  • Bookstore, Food Pantry, Fitness Center: Closed/unavailable until college facilities re-open. (Updated, 3/20/20, 4 p.m.)
  • Tutoring: Online Writing Studio is available through your course in Canvas. Choose the Online Writing Studio tab. (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • Events sponsored by KCC: All college events, including KCC events at off-campus venues, are canceled/postponed until after the Illinois' “shelter in place” order is lifted. Event organizers, including college sponsors of external groups, will inform those who were invited that their event has been canceled. This includes outside organizers renting space at KCC. (Updated, 3/20/20, 4 p.m.)
  • Athletic games and practices: Spring sports seasons (softball and baseball) are canceled. Women's basketball NJCAA Div. II national tournament is canceled. (Updated 3/17/20, 11 a.m.)
  • Employees: Please work with your supervisor to determine if you will work remotely. (Updated, 3/16/20, 1 p.m.)
  • College-sponsored student travel: Suspended. (Updated March 13, 1 p.m. – to include athletics)

Message about Commencement 2020

POSTED ON: 04/06/20

Dear KCC Employees and Students,

Commencement is one of the most important events in the academic year, which is why KCC takes such great care to make our commencement ceremony so special. It is our opportunity, as faculty and staff, to recognize all of the hard work that we do to support students as they work through certificate and degree programs. Most importantly, commencement is a chance for us to recognize the success of the many students who have accomplished so much in KCC’s classrooms, labs, and clinical sites. It’s the special time when students’ families can share in their success and create memories which have lasting, generational impact on peoples’ lives.

COVID-19 has created unprecedented challenges for all of us, and the health of our community requires us to change the way we interact with each other. This means that it will be necessary for us to change the way that we practice commencement this year. Be assured that we will celebrate the success of our May graduates, and we will recognize the hard work that KCC employees continue to do to help our students achieve success. But it will look quite different this year as we celebrate these achievements in an online event.

In the coming weeks, we will have more details to share with you about how we plan to recognize and honor the accomplishments of our graduates online. Though it will be a different kind of commencement than we’ve ever done, I assure you that it will be special. Perhaps we will be able to host a traditional commencement in May of 2021, and we will invite all May 2020 graduates to participate in that ceremony as well so that we can honor your achievements in a more traditional setting.

I realize that this isn’t the way we planned to host commencement this year. Perhaps now more than ever, we need to celebrate our successes and achievements at the end of a very difficult academic year.

Michael Boyd
