UPDATE: Vaccine appointments are full for the KCC distribution site on April 6-7. Appointments are still available for qualified groups on April 5 at Pembroke Fellowship Church. Make an appointment

KCC’s Riverfront Campus has been selected as a Point of Distribution for the COVID-19 vaccine.

KCC's Ryan Gymnasium will be a vaccine distribution site on April 6 and 7 from 9 a.m. until 6 p.m. for qualified groups. Appointments are required.

  • Times/dates of distribution:
    April 6-7 from 9 a.m. until 6 p.m.
  • Registration link:
    Make an appointment

Important information

  • Vaccines will be for those eligible to receive it under Phase 1a, 1b and 1c guidelines.
  • Appointments will be required.
  • It is estimated there will be 700 vaccination appointments per day.
  • The vaccine will be from Johnson & Johnson.
  • Illinois National Guard personnel will be on site to support processing, paperwork, vaccinations and observations.
  • There is no charge to eligible recipients.

On the day of your appointment:

  • Come to KCC’s Riverfront Campus.
  • Follow signs to park and enter the building.
  • Wear a mask and stay physically distanced from others.

Q & A: KCC Point of Distribution

Q: How will I know when I can get the vaccine?

A: Eligibility is based on a phased approach, including phases 1a, 1b, 1c and 2.  For more information on who is eligible in each phase visit the State of Illinois Coronavirus Response website.

Q: What vaccines are available?

A: The Johnson & Johnson vaccine will be available at the KCC point of distribution.

Q: Will I need a follow-up shot?

A: No, the Johnson and Johnson vaccine is a single dose vaccination.

Q: I’m a KCC student. Do I get the vaccine?

A: KCC does not have any influence or control over who receives the vaccine or the time that an individual or group of people may receive the vaccine.

Q: How will KCC serving as a point of distribution affect my classes?

A: This will have no effect on classes.

Q: How much will the vaccine cost?

A: There is no cost for the vaccine.

Q: Is KCC offering COVID-19 testing?

A: No.
